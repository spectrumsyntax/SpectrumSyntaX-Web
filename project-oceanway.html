Ocean WaY

OceanWay is a conceptual maritime technology project focused on improving the daily safety, efficiency, and decision-making of small-scale fishing boats. The project explores how modern digital tools can be adapted to traditional fishing practices without adding complexity or high-cost hardware. OceanWay is designed with the understanding that fishermen operate in challenging environments where connectivity is limited, conditions are unpredictable, and decisions directly affect both safety and income.

Problem Context:

Small fishing boats largely depend on experience and intuition for navigation, fuel usage, and fishing decisions. While this knowledge is valuable, it often lacks real-time awareness of factors such as fuel limits, boundary proximity, and changing sea conditions. Additionally, fishermen face risks such as accidental border crossings, fuel miscalculations, and price uncertainty when selling their catch. Most existing solutions are either too expensive, hardware-heavy, or not designed for real sea conditions.

Core Idea:

OceanWay proposes the idea of a Smart Captain digital assistant that supports fishermen during their trips using a simple, tablet-based system. Instead of replacing human expertise, the system acts as a decision-support layer, providing guidance, alerts, and awareness to help fishermen make safer and more informed choices while at sea. The focus is on reliability, simplicity, and usability rather than complex automation.
